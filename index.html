The rulebook

requirements · essays · how to get in
UC one app covers all 9 campuses · due Nov 30
  • The app: one UC application covers every campus; she checks a box per campus. Opens Aug 1, submit Oct 1 to Nov 30. Fee waivers cover up to 4 campuses for eligible families.
  • Requirements: the 15 "A-G" college-prep courses with at least a 3.0 GPA in them (CA residents). UC recalculates her GPA from 10th-11th grade A-G courses only.
  • Tests and letters: none. UC never sees SAT/ACT scores and takes no recommendation letters.
  • Essays: the 4 PIQs (350 words each), shared by every campus. They are her only voice on the app, so they carry real weight.
  • How to get in: A-G grades first, PIQs second, activities third. Top 9% of her class can earn a guaranteed UC seat (ELC). Choice of major matters at the competitive campuses.
Cal State one app · no essays · due Nov 30
  • The app: Cal State Apply, one application for every CSU. Opens Oct 1, priority deadline Nov 30.
  • Requirements: same A-G courses with at least a 2.5 GPA (CA residents).
  • Tests, letters, essays: none, none, none. The application is the transcript.
  • How to get in: the A-G GPA is nearly the whole game. Hit the priority window; impacted campuses like Long Beach, Fullerton, and Pomona fill up and use higher cutoffs. Local-area applicants get preference at many campuses.
CA privates USC, LMU, Chapman · EA Nov 1
  • The app: Common App, opens Aug 1. Early Action Nov 1, Regular Decision around mid-January (verify each school). USC's Nov 1 date also gates merit scholarships.
  • Requirements: transcript, counselor report, 1-2 teacher letters, school-specific supplements. Test-optional: send scores only if they help her.
  • Essays: the Common App personal statement plus each school's supplements.
  • How to get in: the story carries it: personal statement, supplements, letters. Apply EA. LMU and Chapman track demonstrated interest (open their emails, visit, engage) and both give real merit money.
Out-of-state privates / Ivies REA or ED Nov 1 · RD Jan 1-5
  • The app: Common App. Early is Nov 1: Harvard, Yale, and Princeton allow early at only ONE of them (REA); ED at Columbia, Penn, Brown, Dartmouth, Cornell is binding. Regular Decision Jan 1-5.
  • Requirements: top grades in the hardest available classes, SAT/ACT (required at 6 of 8 Ivies this cycle), 2 teacher letters plus counselor report and mid-year grades, supplements per school, alumni interview at most of them.
  • Essays: personal statement plus heavy per-school supplements. Budget real time for each school; recycled essays read as recycled.
  • Money: no merit scholarships, but the best need-based aid in the country (CSS Profile + FAFSA). For many families an Ivy costs less than a CSU.
  • How to get in: admit rates are 4-8%, so nobody counts on it. What moves the needle: rigor plus scores, a real story told well, and one thing she's exceptional at. If volleyball recruiting is live, an Ivy coach is the single biggest lever there is; coaches champion recruits through admissions. Highlight video to coaches early.

The coaching lane

where her real goal lives
The transfer engine 2 years at a community college: play right away, start coaching, and earn an ADT (Associate Degree for Transfer). Then transfer up: guaranteed into the CSU system on the ADT, and into most UCs via TAG. Finish with a kinesiology or sport-management degree and a college program to coach in. Highest-percentage route to the real goal.
The reframe height closes one door, not the building
  • What height affects: being recruited to play libero at the D1 level (UCs, big CSUs, Ivies), where rosters skew ~5'6"+. At ~4'11", that one door is realistically closed.
  • What it doesn't touch: playing at JUCO, D3 (SCIAC), and NAIA, where skill beats inches, and coaching, which doesn't care about height at all.
  • The point: the goal was always coaching. Playing is how she stays in the gym and learns the game, not the prize.
The ladder how a coaching career actually starts
  • Student manager: get on a college staff as an undergrad; learn film, drills, logistics.
  • Director of volleyball operations: run the behind-the-scenes program.
  • Student / graduate assistant: first real coaching reps while finishing a degree.
  • Assistant coach: the first paid rung. Every program has these roles whether or not she ever played there.
Credential builders start these now, not later
  • USA Volleyball coaching certification (IMPACT / CAP): the entry credential, doable young.
  • Coaching reps: camps, clinics, club, and younger / youth teams. Hours on a whistle are the resume.
  • The degree: kinesiology or sport management aligns the classroom with the career. Strongest at the big CSUs (e.g., Long Beach State, Fresno State).
